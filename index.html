Why me

I was the underweight kid, until I learned to put on lean muscle and keep it.

I know exactly where you're at, because I lived it. Growing up, I was always the smallest, skinniest one in the room. In hockey, my coaches kept telling me I needed to put on size, and no matter what I tried, I couldn't. I did the shakes. I ate everything I could. I ran random programs off the internet. Nothing stuck.

Nobody handed me the answer. I figured it out myself, the hard way, by learning what actually works. It wasn't a shortcut. It was a training program built for my body, showing up consistently, taking recovery as seriously as the training, and following a structured nutrition plan instead of shoveling in random food and hoping. Over two years, that took me from 165 to 225 pounds at 6'5". I didn't get taller. I got built.

Now I coach people through the exact system I spent years piecing together, so you don't have to figure it out alone. That's the whole thing.
